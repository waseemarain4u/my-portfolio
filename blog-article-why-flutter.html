Why Flutter is better?

Flutter, developed by Google, is a popular cross-platform framework known for its numerous advantages over other frameworks. Here's why I believe it stands out.

WA

Waseem Akram

Senior Flutter Developer & Lead Developer

Flutter, developed by Google, is a popular cross-platform framework known for its numerous advantages over other frameworks. After years of building production apps with Flutter across Android, iOS, Web, and Desktop, here are the reasons why Flutter consistently stands out.

  1. 1
    Single Codebase. Flutter allows developers to write a single codebase that runs on iOS, Android, Web, and Desktop. You don't need to maintain separate codebases for different platforms — this significantly reduces development time and effort, and keeps your product consistent everywhere.
  2. 2
    Fast Development. Flutter's hot reload lets you see code changes almost instantly without restarting the app. This dramatically speeds up the development loop — you iterate quickly, fix bugs in real-time, and experiment with UI without losing app state.
  3. 3
    Native Performance. Flutter uses the Skia rendering engine (now Impeller) to draw its own UI directly on the GPU. Apps compile to native ARM code and run directly on the device's hardware — the result is excellent performance and responsiveness that rivals truly native apps.
  4. 4
    Rich UI. Flutter ships with a comprehensive set of customizable widgets that follow both Material Design and Cupertino guidelines. You get beautiful, consistent interfaces across platforms without writing platform-specific UI code.
  5. 5
    Access to Native Features. Flutter provides excellent native integration through its plugin system. Camera, geolocation, Bluetooth, biometrics, push notifications — the pub.dev ecosystem covers virtually every device capability you'll need in a production app.
  6. 6
    Strong Community and Support. Flutter has a large, active developer community with thousands of open-source packages, tutorials, and resources. The community grows every year — finding answers, getting support, and discovering solutions is rarely a problem.
  7. 7
    Cost-effectiveness. One codebase means one team. You don't need separate iOS and Android specialists — a Flutter team delivers to all platforms simultaneously. For startups and agencies, the cost savings compound quickly.
  8. 8
    Future-proofing. Flutter is backed by Google with consistent investment and a clear roadmap. Regular updates, new platform targets, and a stable long-term vision make it a reliable foundation for any serious app project.

While Flutter has numerous advantages, it's important to consider your specific project requirements and the expertise of your development team before choosing any framework. That said, for most cross-platform projects I encounter, Flutter is the right answer.
